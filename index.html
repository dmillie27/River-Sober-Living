Who We Are

Recovery built on
community & purpose

River Sober Living is a men’s sober living community in Boise, Idaho, founded in 2017 with a simple belief: lasting recovery happens in the context of genuine community, personal accountability, and intentional structure.

Situated along the Boise River Greenbelt, our homes offer more than a safe place to sleep. We offer a framework for growth — one that bridges the gap between residential treatment and independent living.

Our residents are mentored, supported, and challenged to build the life skills, healthy habits, and recovery toolkit they need to thrive long after they leave our doors. We partner with Hazelden Betty Ford Foundation as a preferred referral provider.

Men only. Zero tolerance for drugs and alcohol. Open hearts to anyone willing to do the work.

Our Approach

The LEAP Program

LEAP is the foundation of life at River Sober Living — a four-pillar framework designed to guide each resident toward meaningful, sustainable recovery.

“Living, Engaging, Achieving, Persevering.”

L

Living

Learning to live well in sobriety means establishing daily routines, healthy habits, and a stable home environment. We focus on the fundamentals of sober life: sleep, nutrition, exercise, and self-care.

E

Engaging

Recovery doesn’t happen in isolation. Residents engage with their peers, house managers, and the broader recovery community through meetings, group activities, and shared responsibilities.

A

Achieving

We support residents in setting and reaching meaningful goals — employment, education, financial independence, and personal milestones that build confidence and purpose.

P

Persevering

Long-term recovery requires resilience. We equip residents with the coping tools, relapse-prevention strategies, and support network to face challenges and keep moving forward.

+

Structure & Accountability

House rules, regular drug testing, required meetings, and curfews aren’t punishments — they’re the scaffolding that holds early recovery in place while residents build strength.

♡

Community Connection

Residents connect with sponsors, outpatient programs, vocational training, and the greater Boise recovery community. We believe in the village. Nobody does this alone.

House Guidelines

Structure & House Rules

Our guidelines create the conditions for recovery. They are not punitive — they are the container that makes growth possible.

Requirements

  • Active participation in a 12-Step or recovery program
  • Attend a minimum of 3 meetings per week
  • Maintain employment, education, or active job search
  • Weekly house meetings and chores participation
  • Comply with all house curfews and schedules
  • Obtain and work with a sponsor
  • Random drug and alcohol testing at any time
  • Respect for all housemates and house property

House Expectations

  • No drugs, alcohol, or paraphernalia on property at any time
  • No weapons of any kind
  • No guests without prior house manager approval
  • Maintain personal space and shared common areas
  • Pay rent on time and fulfill financial commitments
  • No disruptive, threatening, or abusive behavior
  • Communicate openly with house managers
  • Support your fellow residents in their recovery

Zero Tolerance Policy

River Sober Living maintains a strict zero tolerance policy for drug and alcohol use. Any resident found to be using substances will be asked to leave the house immediately. This policy exists to protect every man in our community and the integrity of the sober living environment.

Common Questions

Frequently
Asked

Have more questions? We’re here to talk. Call us at (208) 576-9527 or send us a message below.

Who is eligible to live at River Sober Living?

+

River Sober Living is for adult men (18+) who are committed to sobriety and seeking a structured, supportive environment. Applicants should have completed or be actively enrolled in a primary treatment program. We accept men at all stages of early recovery.

How long can I live at a River Sober Living house?

+

Length of stay varies by individual. We have no fixed maximum stay — residents remain as long as they are actively working their recovery, meeting house requirements, and benefiting from the community. We work with each resident individually to plan a transition to independence when the time is right.

What does rent include?

+

Rent covers a furnished room in a shared home with common living areas, utilities, Wi-Fi, and access to all LEAP program resources and house manager support. Call or email us for current pricing and availability at each of our four locations.

Do you accept referrals from treatment centers?

+

Yes. We are a Preferred Provider through Hazelden Betty Ford Foundation and work with referrals from treatment centers, hospitals, therapists, and case managers throughout Idaho and the surrounding region. Contact us directly to coordinate a smooth transition for your client.

What happens if a resident relapses?

+

River Sober Living operates a zero tolerance policy. Any resident found using drugs or alcohol will be asked to leave the house. This policy is in place to protect all residents and the integrity of the sober living community. We can assist departing residents in connecting with higher levels of care as needed.

How do I apply or check availability?

+

You can apply using the form on this page, call us at (208) 576-9527, or email marcus@riversoberliving.com. Our hours are Monday through Sunday, 8am–10pm. We’ll respond promptly and guide you through the intake process.

Are meetings required?

+

Yes. All residents are required to attend a minimum of 3 recovery meetings per week (AA, NA, or equivalent). Having an active sponsor and working a program are also requirements of residency. Boise has a vibrant recovery community with meetings available throughout the day and week.
